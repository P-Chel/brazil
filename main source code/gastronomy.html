Culture and History

Get to know the rich culture and history of Brazil.

Feijoada

Considered Brazil’s national dish, Feijoada is a hearty black bean stew simmered with various cuts of pork, such as sausages and ribs. It's traditionally served with rice, sautéed collard greens, orange slices, and farofa (toasted cassava flour).

Pão de Queijo

These are small, chewy cheese breads made from tapioca flour and cheese, offering a delightful snack that's crispy on the outside and soft inside.

Moqueca

A flavorful fish stew originating from Bahia, Moqueca combines fish or seafood with coconut milk, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and coriander, all slowly cooked to perfection.
